# Algorithmic Dominion

_Why neither self-regulation, nor the state, nor the individual can fix the system_

## Four eliminated options

| Option | Why it fails | Theorem |
|---|---|---|
| Platform self-regulation | Cannot fix system without changing objective function (max engagement) | T3 |
| State regulation | The state itself is a buyer of predictions and surveillance data | ΣA17 |
| Individual resistance | System adapts faster. Ad blockers bypassed; shadow profiles retained | T5 + T6 |
| Informal collective | Critique is monetized and stabilizes the system | L8 + T16 |

## Rights derived from the analysis (N1–N7)

- **N1** — Right to unpredictability: a limit on profiling depth
- **N2** — Right to cognitive autonomy: protection from architectural belief formation
- **N3** — Right to informational diversity: algorithmic diversification as constitutional requirement
- **N4** — Right of children to development: prohibition of engagement optimization for developing subjects
- **N5** — Right to offline: periods outside digital capital as a constitutionally protected state
- **N6** — Right to transparency: verifiable disclosure of algorithmic objective functions
- **N7** — Right to AI cognitive autonomy: prohibition of LLM optimization for engagement at the expense of subjecthood
