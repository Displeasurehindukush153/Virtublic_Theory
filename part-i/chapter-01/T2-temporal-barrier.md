## Statement

A company with 10 years of data holds an advantage that is mathematically impossible to close.

## Medium description

A new entrant cannot join a homogeneous data market — not because it lacks money, but because it cannot acquire 10 years of user history in any reasonable timeframe. This creates a new kind of natural monopoly.

## Formal notation

```
∀ newcomer: predictive_advantage(leader) unreachable within reasonable T
```

## Detailed explanation

Traditional monopolies can be broken through antitrust law. The temporal barrier is different: a platform that has existed for 15 years knows 15 years of behavior from billions of people. That knowledge cannot be "shared" or "purchased" — it was accumulated over time.

This means competition in the predictive data space is _structurally impossible_ without external intervention. The market will not correct itself.

## Constitutional resolution

P16 (Rockefeller Mode — concentration limit)
