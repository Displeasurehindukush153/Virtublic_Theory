# Platform Anthropology

_What the system does to the human — and why they cannot defend themselves alone_

## Level of description

Chapter 1 described the mechanism. Now — the subject placed within that mechanism. Its cognitive limitations, psychological vulnerabilities, and why individual resistance is structurally doomed.

## Psychotechnological capture mechanisms

### Variable Reward Schedule
Unpredictable reinforcement — your feed does not show good content consistently, it shows it unpredictably. This is exactly what makes slot machines addictive. The same principle is embedded in pull-to-refresh.

### Social Comparison
Likes, views, followers — numbers constantly visible to the user. They activate social comparison mechanisms that are evolutionarily tuned for continuous attention.

### FOMO (Fear of Missing Out)
Built into notifications, badges, and ephemeral content (stories). This is an architectural decision, not a side effect.

### Infinite Scroll
Absence of a natural stopping point. A book has an end. A newspaper has an end. The feed does not. This is an engineering choice.
