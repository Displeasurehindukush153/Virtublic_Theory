## Statement

You bear the consequences of decisions in which you had no part.

## Medium description

Platform architecture is decided by engineers and managers. You bear the psychological consequences of that architecture. This is the structural asymmetry of the digital economy.

## Formal notation

```
subject bears_consequences(architecture) ∧ subject ¬participated_in(architecture)
```

## Detailed explanation

Variable reward schedules — unpredictable reinforcement — are the same mechanism that makes slot machines addictive. They are used in news feeds. This is an engineering decision. You are the object of that decision, not its author.

FOMO (fear of missing out) is built into the interface deliberately. Social comparison is activated by design. All of this represents architectural choices. Their consequences settle in your mind.
