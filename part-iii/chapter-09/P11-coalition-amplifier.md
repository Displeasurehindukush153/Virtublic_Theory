## Statement

Broad coalitions receive a governance multiplier: influence grows with the number of co-signers, not just capital.

## Formal notation

```
effective_influence(c) = VIC⊥(c) × √(supporters(c))
```

## Detailed description

P11 is the positive complement to P10. While P10 raises the cost of concentration, P11 rewards breadth: effective_influence = VIC⊥(c) × √(supporters(c)).

A proposal backed by 1000 citizens each contributing 1 VIC⊥ achieves effective_influence ≈ 31,623 — versus 5,623 for a single actor with the same total capital. The amplifier is uncapped on the coalition side, structurally shifting power toward distributed participation.

This is the formal operationalization of P0 (popular sovereignty) at the governance level: the system rewards the many over the few not by fiat, but by mathematics.
