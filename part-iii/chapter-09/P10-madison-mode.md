## Statement

Quadratic cost of influence: the greater the concentration of votes, the disproportionately more expensive.

## Formal notation

```
cost(n)=n²; effective_influence=n×√(n_supporters)
```

## Detailed description

10 votes cost 100 VIC⊥ units. 100 votes cost 10,000. Effective influence = n × √(n_supporters). This mathematically rewards broad coalitions and makes concentrated factions economically unviable.

A coalition of 1000 participants is 5.6 times more effective than a single "whale" with the same total VIC⊥. This is the direct answer to T12 (governance capture).
