## Statement

Every blockchain fork is a constitutional moment without a constitution — proof that social consensus precedes code.

## Medium description

When a DAO cannot resolve a conflict through on-chain voting, it defaults to off-chain social consensus: a fork. This proves the opposite of "code is law": the community, not the code, holds final authority.

## Formal notation

```
Crisis(DAO) → Fork → Social_consensus_prevails → Code_is_not_Law
```

## Detailed explanation

The Ethereum/Ethereum Classic split of 2016 is the definitive example. After The DAO hack, the Ethereum Foundation coordinated a hard fork to reverse the theft — an explicit override of immutable code by social consensus.

This is not a failure of blockchain — it is a revelation of its constitutional structure: there always exists a layer of political authority above the code. The question is not whether this layer exists, but whether it is _constitutionally formalized_.

T13 bridges T12 (DAO legitimacy is circular) and T14 (code is law without NA0 = exploitation). It demonstrates that "code is law" is not a factual description but an ideological position — one that collapses under pressure and defaults to the exact politics it claimed to transcend.
