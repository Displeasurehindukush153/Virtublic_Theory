# Blockchain Ontology

_What blockchain does — and what it does not do by its nature_

## The crucial distinction

Blockchain is a technology, not an ideology. This distinction is the foundation of Part II.

| What blockchain does | What blockchain does NOT do |
|---|---|
| Eliminates single point of control | Eliminate inequality (reproduces via PoS) |
| Makes transactions transparent and irreversible | Guarantee democracy (token ≠ citizen vote) |
| Enables rules codified in smart contracts | Protect subjecthood (code is law ∧ ¬NA0 = new arbitrariness) |
| Operates without central trusted agent | — |

## Six axioms of blockchain

### A19 — Decentralization
No single point of control. The political significance of this fact requires separate analysis.

### A20 — Consensus
Trust is transferred to the algorithm. PoW or PoS — trust in a central agent is replaced by trust in the protocol. This does not eliminate trust; it relocates it.

### A21 — Immutability
What is recorded cannot be erased. A guarantee of reliability — and an instrument for entrenching unjust distributions.

### A22 — Tokenization
Any right can be tokenized. And — a new form of concentration: if everything can be tokenized, everything can be purchased.

### A23 — Liquidity
Instant convertibility undermines long-term commitments. A rational holder sells at the first unfavorable vote.

### A24 — Programmability
Smart contracts execute without discretion. Beneficial for predictability. Harmful when the code does not contain NA0 as a base invariant.
